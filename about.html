Our History

Founded in Munich, 1916, BMW (Bayerische Motoren Werke AG) began as an aircraft engine manufacturer. By 1923, BMW produced its first motorcycle, and by 1928 its first car. Today, BMW is a global icon of luxury, innovation, and driving pleasure.

  • 1916: Company founded in Munich, Germany
  • 1923: First BMW motorcycle (R 32)
  • 1928: First car (BMW 3/15)
  • 1972: BMW M division is created
  • 1994: BMW acquires Rover Group
  • 2013: Launch of BMW i (electric mobility)

Global Impact

BMW operates in over 150 countries with 30+ production sites worldwide. The company employs more than 120,000 people and is committed to sustainability, innovation, and social responsibility.

  • BMW Group Plant Rosslyn: First BMW plant outside Germany, based in South Africa
  • Significant contributor to South African economy and exports
